# OBS-004 — Rollback Rate Monitoring

## Objective

Monitor the frequency of compensating actions relative to total transaction volume.

## What to observe

- ratio of rollbacks vs successful transactions
- increase in compensation actions

## What it reveals

- instability in flows
- frequent failure recovery
- systemic issues

## Expected invariants

- rollback rate should remain within expected thresholds
- compensation should be exceptional, not frequent

## Failure signals

- high rollback rate
- sudden spikes in compensation

## Related risks

- Architecture / inadequate design of compensation boundaries
- Architecture / lack of control over compensation responsibility
- Architecture / instability in transaction flow orchestration
- Architecture / excessive reliance on recovery instead of prevention
- Reliability / unstable flows
- Business-financial / transaction inconsistency
- Operational resilience / excessive recovery

## Notes

High rollback rates indicate systemic instability.
